Save the Bees

Cross Media Campaign

Year 4 University Project

The project

The Save the Bees campaign aims to raise awareness about the significant decline of bee populations, and to help the audience take real action. The project uses a range of platforms that are highly immersive. This includes a virtual reality simulation, an information pack, seeds to plant, a website and social media presence. These engage the audience through their interactivity. Together, these platforms inform the audience about the issue of bee-decline, and provide them with tangible, achievable ways to make a positive impact.

What is cross media?

Cross media production enables audiences to more deeply engage with projects, stories and campaigns across a variety of platforms. By creating unique content specifically for certain platforms, and establishing bridges between these platforms, the ‘storyworld’ can become highly immersive, informative and encourage continued audience interest.

The team

This project was completed as a team. I was the project manager, designed the website, info packs and seed packs, coordinated social media and created the documentation. My team members developed storyboards for the VR simulation, created the trailer and also contributed to the documentation. We were invited by the university to present our project at SCANZ2017, an event that focuses on environmental issues and how positive action can be implemented on a larger community scale.
